Guidance and Counseling Services

Academic Counseling

Personalized support to help students achieve their academic goals and overcome learning challenges.

Personal Counseling

Confidential support for personal growth, emotional well-being, and life skill development.

Career Guidance

Comprehensive career counseling to help students explore and prepare for their future paths.

Guidance & Counseling Program and Services

Counseling Service

A guidance service aimed at assisting an individual or group towards self-realization and self-development.

Placement/Career Service

A guidance service that is designed to help clients choose courses, organizations, and activities that fit their needs and interests.

Follow-up Services

A guidance service that aims to follow-up on clients' success or failure in carrying out planned activities after assistance.

Information Service

A guidance service that provides basic and current information on services, opportunities for growth and development, and other related educational information.

Individual Inventory Service

A guidance service that collects, analyzes and interprets pertinent data from clients as bases for due assistance.

Research & Development

Studies and researches about students and how the guidance services and activities are meeting their needs and improving the program for better services.

Objectives of the Guidance & Counseling Program

Student

  • Assist each student in knowing, understanding, and accepting himself, to realize his own unique assets and liabilities and as a result develops a healthy self-concept.
  • Help each student develop normally socially, emotionally, physically, and intellectually and to lead the type of life he desires.
  • Aid each student in attaining appropriate academic as well as vocational development.
  • Help each student to deal with normal as well as complex interpersonal relationships.
  • Assist each student in coping and eventually personal and emotional problems.
  • Aid each student in seeking out and accepting greater amount of responsibility for the direction of his life.

Teacher

  • Help each teacher to provide an optimal learning climate. To do so he must understand each student as well as himself and the subsequent relationship. Thus it is necessary to help each teacher to become cognizant and sensitive to the needs of each individual student.
  • Aid each teacher in identifying (as early as possible) each student's individual uniqueness, including both his assets and liabilities.
  • Assist each teacher in the development of a program of action that focuses on each individual's uniqueness and current needs.
  • Assist each teacher in the classroom instructional program so that it will be more meaningful and relevant for each student.
  • Help each teacher increase the possibility for each student to participate in self-study, problem solving, and decision making.
  • Encourage each teacher to participate in continuous in-service programs emphasizing the psychological growth of the participants. This program will also help teacher realize his role in responsibility to the guidance program.

Community

  • Help the community to provide services, which free and expand, rather than limit, human potential.
  • Assist the community in coordinating all resources that are available to help the student. This involves helping each student become aware of these resources and making sure they remain available.
