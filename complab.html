Computer Laboratory Safety and Workplace Standards

Our computer laboratory is committed to maintaining the highest standards of safety, health, and workplace organization to ensure a productive and secure learning environment.

12 Occupational Health and Safety Protocols:

  • Mandatory personal protective equipment (PPE) assessment and usage
  • Regular safety training and emergency response preparedness
  • Comprehensive risk assessment and hazard identification
  • Strict electrical safety guidelines and equipment maintenance
  • Proper ergonomic workstation setup to prevent physical strain
  • Clear emergency exit routes and evacuation procedures
  • Incident reporting and investigation protocol
  • Health monitoring and wellness programs
  • Chemical and material safety management
  • Noise and environmental comfort controls
  • First aid and medical emergency response system
  • Continuous safety education and awareness programs

5S Clean Workplace Framework:

  • Seiri (Sort): Systematic removal of unnecessary items from workspace
  • Seiton (Set in Order): Organized and efficient arrangement of essential equipment
  • Seiso (Shine): Regular cleaning and maintenance of laboratory spaces
  • Seiketsu (Standardize): Establishing consistent workplace standards and procedures
  • Shitsuke (Sustain): Ongoing commitment to maintaining and improving workplace discipline

Key Features:

  • Dedicated areas for individual and group work
  • Regular maintenance
  • Comprehensive safety and organizational infrastructure
